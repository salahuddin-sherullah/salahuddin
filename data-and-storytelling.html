Overview

Analysis matters more when people can actually follow it.

Good analytical work is not just about getting the numbers right. It is also about organizing the findings, showing what matters, and building outputs that help a team see the pattern without being overwhelmed by detail.

I work across analysis, visualization, and narrative shaping so the final product can support real decision-making rather than sit in a folder as a technically correct but unreadable artifact.

Good fit for

  • Organizations with underused internal data
  • Research teams needing stronger communication
  • Leaders preparing decks, briefs, or donor-facing outputs
  • Teams trying to make dashboards, geospatial analysis, and reporting more useful

What I help with

From raw inputs to clearer stories.

The work can range from one-off analytical products to improving how a team routinely communicates evidence.

Data cleaning and analysis

Working through messy datasets, combining sources, and structuring analysis around the questions that matter most.

Visualization and dashboards

Designing charts, summaries, and custom interactive dashboards that surface patterns without burying the audience in noise.

Complex and geospatial data

Working with large, messy, or spatial datasets so organizations can make better sense of location, distribution, and operational context.

Insight communication

Turning findings into presentations, briefs, and narrative products that connect evidence to context and action.

How I work

Technical enough for the data, practical enough for the audience.

  1. Interrogate the data

    I start by understanding quality, structure, gaps, and what the data can genuinely support.

  2. Shape the analysis

    The analysis is built around decisions and audiences, not just around whatever happens to be easy to calculate.

  3. Communicate the insight

    The final output is translated into visuals and language that help the intended audience actually use it.
