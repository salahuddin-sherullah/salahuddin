GILGIT-BALTISTAN AT A GLANCE

A data-driven overview of Gilgit-Baltistan — Pakistan's northernmost territory — spanning population, geography, and household demographics across its ten districts.

SALAHUDDIN | Feb 2025

Gilgit-Baltistan (GB) is Pakistan's largest administrative territory by area, bordering China, Afghanistan, and the Indian-administered Kashmir. Often called the "roof of the world," it is home to some of the highest mountain peaks on the planet, including K2. Despite its vast and dramatic landscape, GB remains one of the least densely populated regions in South Asia. The data below draws on the 2017 and 2023 censuses to present a snapshot of the territory's population geography.

Population Growth 2017–2023

Each row shows a district's population in 2017 (hollow dot) and 2023 (filled dot), sorted by growth rate. The connecting line spans the change between the two censuses.

The data reveals a territory of stark contrasts: Gilgit city and Diamer host the largest concentrations of people, while Hunza and Kharmang remain sparsely settled despite their geographic scale. The high average household size of 7.33 persons reflects the predominantly rural character of most districts. The sex ratio of 109 — slightly above the national average — is consistent with patterns seen in other mountainous and conservative regions of Pakistan. As GB continues to develop — aided by CPEC infrastructure and growing tourism — understanding these demographic baselines will be critical for equitable planning and resource allocation.

Data source: Pakistan Bureau of Statistics — District Census Results 2023, Gilgit-Baltistan.
