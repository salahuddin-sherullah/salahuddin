Overview

Better strategy usually starts with better questions.

Many teams move too quickly from a broad ambition to a fixed plan. I help slow that process down just enough to define the problem properly, examine what is already known, and identify where evidence is missing or weak.

This work is especially useful when organizations are entering a new issue area, reassessing a program, planning a piece of applied research, or trying to make strategy more grounded in what is actually happening on the ground.

Good fit for

  • Nonprofits refining program direction
  • Research teams scoping new studies
  • Foundations and funders assessing priorities
  • Organizations needing stronger M&E systems before execution

What I help with

Strategy work that stays close to evidence.

I focus on the questions, frameworks, and research decisions that help teams move with more confidence.

Research scoping

Clarifying research questions, audiences, and decisions so a study or learning agenda starts with the right frame.

Strategic diagnosis

Reviewing context, constraints, and available evidence to understand where the real problem sits and what tradeoffs matter.

Program and policy input

Translating findings into practical recommendations that leaders, program teams, and partners can actually use.

M&E frameworks and results indicators

Designing monitoring and evaluation frameworks, results chains, and indicators that keep programs focused on meaningful impact.

How I work

Structured, iterative, and close to the decision.

  1. Frame

    We define the decision, the audience, and the key questions before the work expands into unnecessary complexity.

  2. Investigate

    I synthesize relevant evidence, map assumptions, and identify where more analysis or field input is needed.

  3. Translate

    The final output is built to support action: memo, brief, strategy note, presentation, or research roadmap.
